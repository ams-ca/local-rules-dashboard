# Federal Local Rules Dashboard - Demo Script

**Duration:** 2-3 minutes

---

## Introduction (10 seconds)

"Welcome to the Federal Local Rules Dashboard. This tool helps legal professionals quickly find local rules, standing orders, and judge-specific procedures from federal and state courts across the United States."

---

## Part 1: Federal Court Search (45 seconds)

### Navigate to Federal Courts

**Action:** Click the "State" dropdown

**Script:** "Let's start by searching a federal court. I'll click the State dropdown..."

**Action:** Select "Federal (All Courts)"

**Script:** "...and select 'Federal (All Courts)'. Notice the system shows 8 courts organized by state—4 in California and 4 in New York."

### Select Central District of California

**Action:** Click the "Court" dropdown

**Script:** "Now I'll open the Court dropdown to see all federal district courts grouped by state."

**Action:** Select "Central District of California"

**Script:** "I'll select the Central District of California, one of the busiest federal courts in the nation."

### Submit Search

**Action:** Click the "Search" button

**Script:** "After clicking Search, the system retrieves all available resources for this court."

### Review Federal Court Results

**Action:** Scroll through results

**Script:** "Here we see an AI-generated summary explaining the court's organizational structure, followed by categorized resources: General Orders, Judges, Local Rules, Procedures, and Standing Orders. Each link shows when the URL was last verified, ensuring the information is current."

---

## Part 2: State Court Search (45 seconds)

### Navigate to California State Courts

**Action:** Click the "State" dropdown

**Script:** "Now let's search a state court. I'll select California from the State dropdown."

**Action:** Select "California"

**Script:** "The system now shows 59 California Superior Courts—one for each county."

### Select Monterey County

**Action:** Click the "Court" dropdown and select "Monterey County Superior Court"

**Script:** "I'll select Monterey County Superior Court to demonstrate our comprehensive state court coverage."

### Submit Search

**Action:** Click "Search"

**Script:** "After searching, we see much richer data for state courts."

### Review State Court Results

**Action:** Scroll through results slowly

**Script:** "Notice the expanded categories: Courtroom Guides, Division Rules covering Civil, Criminal, Family, Probate, Traffic, and Small Claims, E-Filing procedures, Judicial Assignments listing all 22 judges, Local Rules, and general Procedures. This comprehensive view gives legal professionals everything they need in one place, with multiple resources per category where applicable."

---

## Part 3: Admin Panel (30 seconds)

### Access Admin Panel

**Action:** Click the "Admin" button in the top-right corner

**Script:** "The Admin button in the top-right opens the management interface."

**Action:** Navigate through the admin panel sections

**Script:** "From here, administrators can preview the live site, browse the code, monitor the dashboard with analytics, manage the database directly with full CRUD operations, and configure settings like domains, notifications, and environment variables. The system also supports GitHub integration for version control and deployment."

---

## Closing (10 seconds)

**Script:** "The Federal Local Rules Dashboard streamlines legal research by aggregating court resources from across the country into a single, searchable interface. Whether you're researching federal district courts or state superior courts, all the information you need is just a few clicks away."

---

## Key Talking Points

- **Two-tier selection:** Federal vs. State distinction makes navigation intuitive
- **Comprehensive coverage:** Multiple resource types per court (not just local rules)
- **State court depth:** Division-specific rules, judicial assignments, e-filing procedures
- **URL verification:** All links show last verification date for reliability
- **AI summaries:** Contextual explanations help users understand court structure
- **Admin capabilities:** Full management interface for maintaining and expanding the database
- **Scalability:** Architecture supports adding more states and courts easily

---

## Demo Tips

1. **Pace yourself:** Allow time for pages to load and users to see results
2. **Highlight differences:** Emphasize the richer data available for state courts (Monterey) vs. federal courts
3. **Point out details:** Show the "URL Verified" dates and "AI Generated" disclaimer
4. **Mention scale:** Reference the 59 CA Superior Courts and 8 federal district courts currently in the system
5. **Future vision:** Briefly mention plans to expand to all 50 states
